Mobile Browsing - Analysis

Introduction

I was tasked with outside the need for homework to use my phone when viewing webpages. Unfortuanely for the assignmnet I only used needed to view one site.

Questions to consider:

  • Can you still see what you want to see?
  • Can you find what you're looking for?
  • Can you do what you want to do?
  • Any frustrations?
  • Anything work better on the small screen?

Findings

Can you still see what you want to see?

I was still able to see what I needed to. I used My Fridge Food to look up a recipe for a cake in a mug. I was still able to search my ingredients, like on the desktop version.

Can you find what you're looking for?

The results page from my search for a recipe was setup as a list. It was difficult to scroll through the results, but I found a recipe still.

Can you do what you want to do?

I was able to find a recipe fairly quickly and reading the instructions was not difficult like other sites I have view previously.

Any frustrations?

The main problem was that every page was slow to load.

Anything work better on the small screen?

I like being able to look up recipes on my phone versus desktop, because my phone is more moveable, but My Fidge food is not a reccomened site for mobile.

Summary

I learned that while it might be perferd for a service to be delievered no mobile it is not always the best format for it. I was surprised on how difficult going throught the results were. It was a lot to look through a too small screen.
